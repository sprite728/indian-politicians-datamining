Special Interests(i) Eradication of poverty, illiteracy, ill health, food crisis, housing problems, drinking water crisis of Indian population; (ii)facilitate people`s movement towards change over of present society to a exploitation free socialist society; and (iii) study literature of Shibdas Ghosh, founder of Socialist Unity Centre of India (SUCI) and Marxian classics
Sports and ClubsParticipated and represented college football and cricket teams; sports like sprint, long jump and high jump; free hand exercises and walking
Favourite Pastimes and RecreationMedical social work to provide free medical services, especially at times of disaster for distressed and deprived people, watch good games/sports items, good films, dramas,listening music/songs and study literature, history and journals and `adda` with close friends
Other InformationVoluntarily retired from the post of Chief Medical Officer (Selection Grade), ISPH, Nashik in july 2007;Participated in (i) disaster management works such as rendering free medical services as member and leader of Medical Service Centre, a socio-medical voluntary organisation functioning at different places for the last 32 years namely West Bengal floods, Assam floods, Supercyclone Orissa, Kosi flood Bihar, Mumbai flood (2005), Baroda and Surat floods (2005-06), Tsunami, Andaman Nicobar Islands, Earthquake, Gujrat, Aila Cyclone, Sunderbans, West Bengal, Kolkata and Mumbai slums: (ii) Health movements organised for improvement of medical education, health care delivery system of West Bengal and India; (iii)participated in the people`s resistance movement of Singur and Nandigram against land grabbing; and (iv) working for protection and upliftment of medical ethics and for pro-people scientific health policies;
